What do confidence intervals between two groups tell us?

When comparing two groups, you can look at their individual confidence intervals or create a single CI around the mean difference. This tool shows you both.

How to use this tool: Adjust the mean, standard deviation, and sample size for each group using the sliders. The top section shows the individual 95% CIs for each group. The bottom section shows how those two CIs translate into a single CI around the mean difference.
How to interpret: If the CI of the mean difference does not include zero, the difference between groups is statistically significant. If it does include zero, the difference is not statistically significant. Notice how the individual CIs and the CI of the difference always tell the same story.
