How do we determine if a t-statistic is significant?

Every t-test produces a t-statistic that must exceed a threshold (t-critical) to be considered significant. In this high jump, the bar is t-critical and the jumper is your t-statistic.

How to use this tool: Adjust degrees of freedom (i.e., the number of participants in the study) and the t-statistic using the sliders. Watch whether the jumper clears the bar or not.
How to interpret: If the jumper clears the bar (i.e., the t-statistic exceeds the t-critical value), the result is statistically significant (p < .05). If the jumper hits the bar (i.e., the t-statistic does not exceed the t-critical value), it is not statistically significant. Notice that as degrees of freedom increase (larger samples), the bar drops — meaning it takes a smaller t-statistic to reach significance (and vice versa).
