Descriptive statistics (Unit 2)
Effect size
Use this tool to explore what Cohen's d actually looks like. Drag the slider to change the effect size and watch the treatment group separate from the control group. The overlap percentage tells you how much the two groups still share in common.
Balancing statistical significance and clinical meaningfulness
Use this tool to better understand how to balance between statistical significance (i.e., p-values, effect sizes) and clinical meaningfulness (i.e., clinical benchmarks, MCID). Toggle three questions and watch which quadrant lights up.
Comparing two groups or conditions (Unit 3)
What is a confidence interval?
Cast a net by adjusting the sample size and see if you can find the golden egg (i.e., the population mean). The 95% CI is your net — a larger sample gives you a narrower, more precise net.
Interpreting confidence intervals between two groups
Use this tool to create confidence intervals for two groups. Then interpret the CIs in two ways: comparing the two CIs directly or creating a CI around the mean difference.
The t-test high jump
See how degrees of freedom change the t-critical value. The jumper is your t-statistic, the bar is t-critical — can you clear it?
Comparing three or more groups or conditions (Unit 4)
Selecting the correct significance test
Answer two questions — how many groups and how many conditions — and the interactive flowchart guides you to the correct statistical test.
The F-test high jump
The same high jump concept applied to ANOVA. Adjust the number of groups and total participants to see how F-critical changes. The purple jumper is your F-statistic — can you clear the bar?
The Bonferroni limbo
This tool illustrates how the alpha level is adjusted based on the number of comparisons needed for multiple comparisons. Each stick figure's height represents a p-value. The limbo bar is the Bonferroni-adjusted alpha based on the number of comparisons. Watch what happens as you add more groups — the bar drops and fewer comparisons survive.
Putting all of the F-test pieces together
Use this tool to gain a better understanding from omnibus test to multiple comparisons to Bonferroni adjustments. Walk through each decision step and see which pairwise comparisons survive the adjusted threshold.

Capstone Activity
The Analysis Pipeline
Work through the complete statistical analysis pipeline from start to finish. Choose from four clinical scenarios (audiology and OT), then answer questions at every step — identify the study design, select the correct test, interpret results, and determine whether post-hoc comparisons are needed. Covers t-tests, confidence intervals, ANOVA, RMANOVA, degrees of freedom, and Bonferroni correction all in one guided walkthrough.
