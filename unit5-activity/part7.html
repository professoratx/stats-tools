Step 1 · Re-read with fresh eyes
Custom musician earplugs and 12-month risk of measurable hearing loss in young adults
Results

At 12 months, 25 of 100 patients (25%) in the earplug group developed measurable hearing loss, compared with 55 of 100 patients (55%) in the no-protection group. Compared with no protection, custom earplug use was associated with a statistically significant reduction in measurable hearing loss (RR = 0.45, 95% CI [0.30, 0.68], p < .001). The absolute reduction in 12-month risk was 30 percentage points (ARR = 30%, 95% CI [18%, 42%]), corresponding to NNT = 4 (95% CI [3, 6]). The odds of developing measurable hearing loss were substantially lower in the earplug group (OR = 0.27, 95% CI [0.15, 0.50], p < .001).

Conclusions

In this 12-month controlled study, consistent use of custom musician earplugs was associated with a clinically and statistically significant reduction in measurable hearing loss among young adults working at high-volume music venues. Findings support fitting and recommendation as a routine component of audiological practice for at-risk occupational groups.
