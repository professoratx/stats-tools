What you're looking at: This tool lets you move sliders to change the outcome rates for two groups and instantly see how RR, ARR, and NNT respond. The key insight: Relative Risk (RR) tells you how much bigger one rate is compared to another, but it hides the actual size of the gap. Two studies can both report "RR = 2.0" and have completely different clinical implications. In the mock article, RR = 2.20 sounds impressive — but it's the NNT of 4 that tells you this treatment truly matters. Select "Treatment vs. Control" and any audiology scenario to begin.
